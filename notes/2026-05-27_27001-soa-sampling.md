# 27001 Runtime — SoA-Driven Annex A Sampling (BWOC-34)

Captures the implementation decisions for replacing `audit-iso-27001/audit.sh` stub with a runnable ISMS runtime — the **last** EPIC-3 stub, closing the epic. 27001 is the only standard that needs **both** evidence kinds *and* a dynamic, operator-declared population: the [Statement of Applicability](https://www.iso.org/standard/27001) (SoA, clause 6.1.3) decides which Annex A controls are in scope, and the runtime samples from that set rather than from a fixed catalogue.

The attestation half reuses BWOC-28 (9001) verbatim; the sample mechanics reuse BWOC-33 (20000-1). The new wrinkle — anticipated by the [20000-1 note](2026-05-27_20000-1-sample-source.md#open-follow-ups-out-of-scope-for-bwoc-33) ("Annex A sampling is SoA-driven, which is its own wrinkle — a dynamic population") — is **where the sample population comes from**. No dispatcher change is needed: `crates/bwoc-cli/src/audit.rs` already validates `attestation`, `sample`, and the `not_applicable` status (BWOC-28/29).

## Decision 1 — Evidence-kind split across the eight criteria

The eight `criterion_id`s are STABLE (carried over unchanged from the v0.1.0 stub — PLUGINS.en.md §Stability). The split follows the same rule as 20000-1 — **documented-artifact → `attestation`, operational/sampled → `sample`** — and matches the [BWOC-26 matrix](2026-05-27_iso-runtime-evidence-model.md#standard--evidence-kind-matrix) ("attestation (5.2, 6.1.2, 6.1.3) + sample (Annex A controls)").

| `criterion_id` | Reference | Kind | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| `27001-isms-scope` | 4.3 | **attestation** | Scope is a documented artifact the operator vouches for. No population to sample. |
| `27001-information-security-policy` | 5.2 | **attestation** | Called out explicitly in BWOC-26 / PLUGINS.en.md as the 27001 attestation clause. |
| `27001-risk-assessment` | 6.1.2 | **attestation** | A defined, applied risk-assessment process — an operator-signed assertion that it exists and was run. |
| `27001-statement-of-applicability` | 6.1.3 | **attestation** | Top management attests the SoA is established and maintained (see Decision 3 for the SoA's *second* role as the sampling population). |
| `27001-access-control` | A.5.15 | **sample** | Annex A control. Sampled from the in-scope control set per the SoA. |
| `27001-incident-management` | A.5.24 | **sample** | Annex A control. Sampled from the in-scope control set. |
| `27001-business-continuity` | A.5.29 | **sample** | Annex A control. Sampled from the in-scope control set. |
| `27001-internal-audit` | 9.2 | **attestation** | Internal audits conducted at planned intervals — an operator-signed assertion, dated. |

**Result: 5 attestation (main-body clauses 4–10) + 3 sample (Annex A controls).** The split tracks the structural divide in ISO/IEC 27001:2022 itself: the main-body management-system clauses (4–10) are documented-artifact conformance; the 93 Annex A controls are the technical/organisational measures an auditor *samples*. Per-criterion kind is declared in `criteria.toml` via `expected_evidence_kind` + the BWOC-29 per-kind `required` subtable — `bwoc check` validates it statically; `audit.sh` reads it at invoke time and routes.

## Decision 2 — The SoA as the sampling population (the new wrinkle)

20000-1's samples are self-contained: the operator types `sampled_count` / `sampled_of` directly per sample (e.g. "49 of 50 incidents"). 27001 is different — the BWOC-26 note frames Annex A sampling as *"we sampled controls A.5.15, A.5.24, A.5.29 (3 of 37) without inflating the finding count to 37."* The population (`sampled_of`) is **not** a number the operator types per control; it is the size of the **in-scope control set**, which the SoA defines.

So the runtime needs a machine-readable SoA. The operator declares one in `workspace.toml`:

```toml
[plugins.audit-iso-27001]
enabled = true

# Statement of Applicability — one entry per Annex A control the operator
# has assessed. ISO/IEC 27001 6.1.3 requires a justification for BOTH
# inclusions and exclusions.
[[plugins.audit-iso-27001.soa]]
control       = "A.5.15"
applicable    = true
justification = "Access control is central to protecting source, credentials, and customer data."

[[plugins.audit-iso-27001.soa]]
control       = "A.5.29"
applicable    = false
justification = "No formal continuity programme; risk accepted by management for a solo workspace."
```

- **In-scope control set** = SoA entries with `applicable = true`. Let `M` = its size (`sampled_of`).
- **Plugin sampling breadth** `K` = how many of *this plugin's* three Annex A controls are `applicable = true` in the SoA. `K ≤ M` by construction (each in-scope plugin control is itself counted in `M`).

This is the **SoA-driven** part: `sampled_of` is computed from the SoA, never typed by the operator. It tracks the operator's scope decisions automatically — exclude a control and `M` shrinks; the audit's reported denominator follows.

### Why `K of M`, computed by scope (not by evidence completeness)

Each Annex A **pass** finding reports the same audit-wide rate `sampled_count = K`, `sampled_of = M`, with its own `value` summary naming the specific control. This reproduces the BWOC-26 narrative ("we sampled 3 of 37") as the aggregate of three `K`-of-`M` findings.

`K` counts in-scope controls **by scope** (applicable=true), independent of whether each has a recorded sample. That keeps findings **decoupled** (BWOC-11: "a criterion passes or fails as a unit; no nested findings"): one control's `sampled_count` never depends on a sibling control's evidence. An in-scope control that is missing its sample **fails** (its absence is visible in the fail finding) rather than silently deflating `K` on the others.

## Decision 3 — The SoA's two roles

The SoA appears twice, and the two uses are intentionally **independent**:

1. **Attestation criterion `27001-statement-of-applicability` (6.1.3)** — routed through `[[plugins.audit-iso-27001.attestations]]` like any other attestation. Top management attests "the SoA is established and maintained," signed and dated.
2. **The `[[plugins.audit-iso-27001.soa]]` array** — the machine-readable in-scope declarations that drive Annex A sampling (Decision 2).

They are complementary (an operator who maintains a real SoA naturally does both) but not coupled in code — the attestation passes/fails on its own evidence; the soa array drives the three Annex A criteria on its own. Coupling them ("fail the 6.1.3 attestation if the soa array is empty") was rejected: it violates the flat, independent-finding model and would make one finding's status depend on another's input. The SPEC notes the natural pairing for operators without enforcing it.

## Decision 4 — Pass / not_applicable / fail semantics for Annex A criteria

Presence-based, mirroring 20000-1 (no organisational threshold is hard-coded). For each of the three Annex A criteria, resolve its `annex_control` (declared in `criteria.toml`) and look it up in the SoA:

| SoA state for the control | Status | Evidence | Remedy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Absent from SoA** | `fail` | `file` → `.bwoc/workspace.toml` | "6.1.3 requires the SoA to address every Annex A control. Declare `control`, `applicable`, `justification`." |
| **`applicable = false`, no justification** | `fail` | `file` | "6.1.3 requires a justification for exclusions. Add `justification`." |
| **`applicable = false`, justified** | `not_applicable` | `none` | "Excluded per SoA: \"<justification>\". Re-confirm at the next audit cycle." (remedy is required for `not_applicable`.) |
| **`applicable = true`, no justification** | `fail` | `file` | "6.1.3 requires a justification for inclusions too. Add `justification`." |
| **`applicable = true`, no sample entry** | `fail` | `file` | "In scope but no recorded audit sample. Add `[[…samples]]` with `criterion_id` + `summary`." |
| **`applicable = true`, sample missing `summary`** | `fail` | `file` | names the missing field. |
| **`applicable = true`, justified, sample present** | `pass` | `sample` (`value` = summary, `sampled_count` = K, `sampled_of` = M, optional `window`) | — |

`not_applicable` is the correct ISO outcome for a justifiably-excluded control — it is neither a pass (we did not test it) nor a fail (the operator made a defensible scoping decision). The schema allows `evidence.kind = "none"` only with `not_applicable` / `not_implemented`, and requires a `remedy` for `not_applicable` — both honoured.

The fail-path `evidence.kind = "file"` pointing at `.bwoc/workspace.toml` is the same honest shape BWOC-28/33 settled on: a missing-evidence finding cannot use `kind = "none"` (forbidden with `fail`) nor an empty-value `sample`. The file the operator must edit *is* the reproducible referent.

### The Annex A sample entry — thinner than 20000-1

Because `sampled_count` / `sampled_of` are SoA-derived, the operator's sample entry only records *that the control was sampled* plus a human summary:

```toml
[[plugins.audit-iso-27001.samples]]
criterion_id = "27001-access-control"
summary      = "Access reviews completed across in-scope systems; 0 orphaned accounts found."
window       = "2026-Q1"   # optional
```

`criterion_id` + `summary` required; `window` optional. The operator does **not** supply `sampled_count` / `sampled_of` here (contrast 20000-1) — supplying them would let the operator's hand-typed denominator drift from the SoA-derived one, defeating the point of SoA-driven sampling.

## How the runtime works (target shape)

```bash
bwoc audit run --plugin audit-iso-27001 --json
```

1. Dispatcher spawns `audit.sh` with `BWOC_WORKSPACE`, `BWOC_PLUGIN_DIR`, `BWOC_AUDIT_OPERATION`.
2. `audit.sh` reads `criteria.toml` → per criterion: `id`, `severity`, `expected_evidence_kind`, and (for Annex A criteria) `annex_control`.
3. `audit.sh` reads `.bwoc/workspace.toml`, builds three lookup tables: `attestations`, `samples` (both keyed by `criterion_id`), and `soa` (keyed by `control`). Computes `M` and `K` from the SoA up front.
4. For each criterion in declaration order, routes by `expected_evidence_kind`:
   - `attestation` → identical to 20000-1 (present+complete → pass; incomplete/absent → fail file).
   - `sample` → SoA-gated per Decision 4.
5. Dispatcher validates findings against the BWOC-27 schema, emits the canonical envelope. Exit code = `fail` count (BWOC-12); `not_applicable` does not count as a fail.

## Stability

All 8 `criterion_id`s are unchanged from v0.1.0. Adding `expected_evidence_kind`, the per-kind `required` subtables, and the documentation-only `annex_control` field to `criteria.toml` is additive. Plugin version `0.1.0 → 0.2.0` (minor — contract grows, nothing renamed; PLUGINS.en.md §Stability).

## Alternatives considered

- **Operator types `sampled_of` per Annex A sample (20000-1 shape).** Rejected — lets the denominator drift from the SoA; "SoA-driven" means the scope decision *is* the population. The whole point of the BWOC-26 framing was to avoid 93 hand-maintained numbers.
- **One finding per in-scope Annex A control (all 93).** Rejected — inflates the finding count, exactly what BWOC-26 warned against. The plugin samples three representative controls; `K of M` records the sampling fraction.
- **`applicable = false` → `pass`.** Rejected — passing implies "we tested it and it's fine." An excluded control was *not* tested; `not_applicable` is the honest status (Musāvāda).
- **Couple the 6.1.3 attestation to a non-empty soa array.** Rejected — breaks flat, independent findings (Decision 3).

## Open follow-ups (out of scope for BWOC-34)

- **`bwoc check` validation of the workspace `[[soa]]` array** (well-formed `control` refs, `applicable` is bool, justification present). Currently BWOC-29 validates `criteria.toml`, not `workspace.toml` — same L2 gap noted for 20000-1.
- **Full Annex A catalogue.** v0.2.0 samples three A.5 (Organizational-theme) controls. A future minor bump could declare representative controls from A.6 (People), A.7 (Physical), A.8 (Technological) — additive, no id renames.
- **Control-to-clause traceability.** A.5.24 feeds both clause 9 and clause 10; deferred by BWOC-26 to a possible v3 `evidence.related_criteria`.
