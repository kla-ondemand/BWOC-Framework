# Code of Conduct

This Code is grounded in two Buddhist frameworks already used throughout BWOC for engineering reasoning: **Sīla 5** (the Five Precepts) for prohibited conduct, and **Brahmavihāra 4** (the Four Divine Abidings) for expected disposition.

Pali terms are section names. Content is technical. This Code does not endorse, require, or assume any religious affiliation — consistent with the framework's non-goals (see [`VISION.md`](VISION.md#non-goals)).

---

## Sīla 5 — Prohibited Conduct

Five behaviors are out of bounds anywhere this Code applies. Each is the engineering translation of a precept.

1. **Pāṇātipātā veramaṇī** — *no harm.* No harassment, threats, doxxing, hate speech, or personal attacks. Disagree with the work, not the person.
2. **Adinnādānā veramaṇī** — *no taking what is not given.* Respect attribution, license, and intellectual property. No plagiarism. No claiming credit for someone else's work.
3. **Kāmesumicchācārā veramaṇī** — *no improper conduct.* No unwanted advances, sexualized comments, or sexualized imagery in project channels. Respect stated boundaries.
4. **Musāvādā veramaṇī** — *no false speech.* No impersonation, falsified results, misleading commit messages, manufactured consensus, or fabricated quotes.
5. **Surāmerayamajjapamādaṭṭhānā veramaṇī** — *no heedlessness.* Do not contribute under impaired judgment. No reckless commits that bypass review. Stop and resume when you can maintain the other four.

## Brahmavihāra 4 — Expected Disposition

Four positive dispositions are expected by default. They are the same four already named in [`modules/agent-template/AGENTS.md`](modules/agent-template/AGENTS.md) §3.3 for agent-to-user tone, applied here to contributor-to-contributor tone.

1. **Mettā** — *friendly directness.* Speak plainly. Be the reviewer you want reviewing your own work.
2. **Karuṇā** — *suggest a fix, not only the error.* When you find a mistake, propose a path forward when you can.
3. **Muditā** — *acknowledge what is right.* Credit good ideas explicitly. Welcome first-time contributors.
4. **Upekkhā** — *stay even.* Disagree without escalating. Step away before responding to provocation.

---

## Scope

This Code applies in:

- This repository's issues, pull requests, discussions, and review comments.
- Any maintainer-operated channel that links back to this project.

It does not apply in private communication outside the project, except where such communication is explicitly conducted in a maintainer capacity.

---

## Reporting

Report a concern to **info@bemind.tech**. Include what happened, where, and (if applicable) links.

Maintainers decide the outcome on a case-by-case basis. The project does not currently publish a fixed enforcement ladder.

---

## Note on Framing

Sīla 5 and Brahmavihāra 4 are used here as **engineering thinking aids** — a coherent vocabulary for community conduct that fits the rest of the BWOC technical stack. They are not, and are not intended as, religious instruction. Contributors of any background, faith, or non-faith are welcome.
